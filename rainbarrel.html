Water Filtering Rain Barrel
with Solar LCD Display.

Team Report for Project

I completed this project during my Summer 2022 semester at RPI.
The class was called Introduction to Engineering Design, and
I was assigned a random team to create a project that would serve
a functional purpose to any audience we wanted,
but it had to include several aspects of engineering.
In this report, we discuss each of our subsystems and much more.
You can also find our "team-building" exercises, where we took time to
develop not only professional but also personal
relationships. This is what makes working in a team fun,
learning about each other and each person's individual skillset.
Please read if you are interested.

Initial Circuit Schematic

I worked alongside one of my group partners to
figure out how the wiring for the Arduino microcontroller(the brain of the circuit)
would interact with the necessary sensors and the breadboard. Some of the components include
a pH sensor, a turbidity sensor, and load cells.
While the former two are easier to understand, the latter(load cells) were implemented
to measure pressure, and using the ideal Gas Law, we would be able to use to determine volume.
This would allow the circuit to let us know how full our barrel was.
Although not included in the schematic, the solar panels were also
connected to the circuit, and I wired them in series to get the most optimal voltage reading.

Wiring and testing the load cells

In this picture, I am testing the load cells with a partner, and the expected output,
if wired properly, of the load cell E+/E- and A+/A- diagonal terminals would be around 2000 ohms.
As seen in the picture, we got this as our result.
Unfortunately, the load cells were faulty and did not give us proper results.
Our team is still not sure what caused them to fail, but it there have been a fault during their manufacturing.
The setup and code was researched and executed well. Despite this failure, we had to move on.

The LCD working

After wiring, it was time to Code. Using libraries such as the
LiquidCrystal library for the LCD in the Arduino IDE,
and doing some math,our output was succesful.
Although our load cells failed, we still had
important data that came out well.
And just to add, the water filtration subsystem of our project was very
succesful.
