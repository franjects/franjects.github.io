In this project, I first had to get the resistance of the
thermistor using the voltage divider equation, but
rearranged to get R2, the thermistor rating. Of course I had to
remember that voltage was calculated differently in the arduino, so
I used the above equation to account for this.

Steinhart-Hart Equation Calculations

I then had to find the resistance ratings of the thermistor for 3 different temperatures.
To simulate drastically different temperatures, I simply used different
different temperatures of water, using a thermometer to check.
In this case, I determined:

T1 = 5 C, R1 = 28400 Ohms
T2 = 17 C, R2 = 11400 Ohms
T3 = 40 C, R3 = 10 Ohms

After this I would have to plug these values into the equation in the above figure.
I then used Matlab to solve the system of equations, which led to:

A = 3.293 * 10^-3
B = -4.7 * 10^-5
C = 7.2899 * 10^-7
